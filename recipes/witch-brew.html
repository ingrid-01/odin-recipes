A Witch's Brew

By William Shakespeare


Description

In the classic Shakespeare tragedy, Macbeth, the three witches gather some weird mix of ingredients to make..well, who-knows-what.

This brew eggs Macbeth on to encourage his ambition, which ultimately leads to his demise.

So...Who's up for a shot?

Ingredients

  • A fillet of a fenny snake
  • An eye of a newt (or some mustard seeds)
  • A toe of a frog (or some buttercup flowers)
  • A wool of bat (or some holly leaves)
  • A tongue of a dog (or a gympsyflower from the Genus Hound's Tounge)
  • An adder's fork (or a bunch of Least Adder's tongue)
  • A blind-worm's sting
  • A Lizard's leg
  • An owlet's wing
  • A drop of a baboon's blood

Steps

  1. First! Heat up you caldron. Double, double toil and trouble; Fire burn and caldron bubble.
  2. Then start adding your ingredients! Fillet of a fenny snake, In the caldron boil and bake;
  3. Stir them as you gently boil them. Eye of newt and toe of frog, Wool of bat and tongue of dog,
  4. Turn up the heat! Adder's fork and blind-worm's sting, Lizard's leg and howlet's wing
  5. Let it boil for 10 minutes on high heat. For a charm of powerful trouble, Like a hell-broth boil and bubble.
  6. Turn the heat down, and simmer for another 1-2 minutes. Double, double toil and trouble; Fire burn and caldron bubble.
  7. Cool down the brew and serve! Cool it with a baboon's blood, Then the charm is firm and good


William Shakespeare. Macbeth: IV.i 10-19; 35-38
